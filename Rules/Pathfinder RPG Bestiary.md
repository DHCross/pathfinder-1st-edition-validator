* **Hit Points**  
* **Armor Class (AC)**  
* **Attack Bonus** (Use "High Attack" for combat-focused monsters, "Low Attack" for monsters that rely on spells or abilities)

* \[cite\_start\]**Physical Scores (Str, Dex, Con):** These should be relatively close to the base values listed in **Table 1‚Äì3: Size**\[cite: 4083\].  
* **Mental Scores (Int, Wis, Cha):** These are defined by your concept. \[cite\_start\]The baseline is 10\. Creatures with an Intelligence of 2 or lower are generally mindless or incapable of speech\[cite: 4083\].

**Step 6: Skills and Feats**

* **Skills:** Use **Table 1‚Äì4: Creature Statistics by Type** (page 293\) to find the number of skill ranks your monster gets per Hit Die. \[cite\_start\]Assign these ranks based on its concept and its type's class skills\[cite: 4083, 4081\].

### A Guide to Hit Dice (HD) for Creatures and NPCs

In Pathfinder 1st Edition, "Hit Dice" is the foundation of a creature's or character's statistics. A Hit Die represents a "level" of experience or innate power. It determines a creature's Hit Points (HP), Base Attack Bonus (BAB), base saving throw bonuses, skill ranks, and the total number of feats it possesses.

Understanding "proper" HD means knowing which *type* of Hit Dice to use. There are three primary ways HD is determined in Pathfinder.

---

### 1\. Player Characters (PC) & "Heroic" NPCs

This is the most straightforward method, detailed in the *Core Rulebook*, *Advanced Player's Guide*, and *Advanced Class Guide*.

4. **How HD is Determined:** By **Class Level**.  
5. **Die Type:** Depends on the specific class.  
   * **d6:** "Fragile" classes (e.g., Sorcerer, Wizard).  
   * **d8:** "Versatile" classes (e.g., Bard, Cleric, Druid, Rogue).  
   * **d10:** "Martial" classes (e.g., Fighter, Paladin, Ranger).  
   * **d12:** The "Toughest" class (Barbarian).  
6. **What it Governs:**  
   * **HP:** At 1st level, PCs and "Heroic" NPCs get the maximum die roll. Every level after, they roll (or take the average) and add their Constitution modifier.  
   * **BAB & Saves:** Determined by the class's progression (fast, medium, or slow).  
   * **Skill Ranks:** Determined by the class \+ Intelligence modifier.  
   * **Feats:** 1 feat at 1st level, and another at every odd-numbered level (3rd, 5th, etc.).

**When to use this:**

7. For all Player Characters (PCs).  
8. For major villains, rivals, or important allies who are built with the same rules as PCs.

---

### 2\. Monsters (Racial Hit Dice \- RHD)

This method is the focus of the *Bestiary*. Most monsters do not have class levels. Their power comes from their innate nature.

9. **How HD is Determined:** By **Racial Hit Dice (RHD)**.  
10. **Die Type:** Depends on the creature's **Type**. This is a critical rule, found in the *Bestiary* appendices.  
    * **d6:** Fey, Outsider (native)  
    * **d8:** Aberration, Animal, Dragon, Giant, Humanoid, Magical Beast, Ooze, Plant, Undead, Vermin  
    * **d10:** Construct, Outsider (non-native)  
11. **What it Governs:**  
    * **HP:** Roll all RHD and add the creature's Con modifier for *each* die. (Monsters do not get max HP at 1st level unless their first HD is from a PC class).  
    * **BAB & Saves:** Determined by the RHD and the chassis of the creature's Type (e.g., Humanoids have "medium" BAB and good Reflex saves; Undead have "medium" BAB and good Will saves).  
    * **Skill Ranks:** Determined by the RHD and the creature's Type \+ Int modifier.  
    * **Feats:** 1 feat for the first RHD, and another for every 2 RHD after that (3 HD, 5 HD, 7 HD, etc.).

**When to use this:**

12. For all standard monsters found in the *Bestiary* (e.g., Goblins, Dragons, Golems, Zombies).  
13. When "Advancing" a monster (as rules in the *Bestiary*): You can add more RHD to a base monster to make it a tougher "leader" or "alpha" version.

---

### 3\. "Simple" NPCs (NPC Classes)

This method is detailed in the *GameMastery Guide*. It's used for the vast majority of unnamed characters in the world who aren't adventurers.

14. **How HD is Determined:** By **NPC Class Level**.  
15. **Die Type:** Depends on the specific NPC class.  
    * **d6:** Commoner, Expert  
    * **d8:** Adept, Aristocrat  
    * **d10:** Warrior  
16. **What it Governs:**  
    * **HP:** Roll (or take average) for each level and add Con modifier.  
    * **BAB & Saves:** All NPC classes have a "slow" BAB progression and a specific "good" save (e.g., Warrior has good Fortitude, Expert has good Reflex).  
    * **Skill Ranks:** Determined by the class.  
    * **Feats:** 1 feat at 1st level, and another at every odd-numbered level.

**When to use this:**

17. For creating guards, shopkeepers, cultists, nobles, or any other "background" character.  
18. These classes are intentionally weaker than PC classes to make them easy to run and keep the focus on the heroes.

---

### The Most Important Rule: Combining HD

This is the advanced step, combining **Path 1** and **Path 2**.

A creature's **Total Hit Dice** is the *sum* of its **Racial Hit Dice** and its **Class Levels**.

19. **Total HD \= RHD \+ Class Levels**

This "Total HD" is what you use to determine its total feats, skill rank caps, and ability score increases (one point every 4 HD).

**Example: A Bugbear Fighter**

1. **Base Monster (Path 2):** A standard Bugbear (from the *Bestiary*) is a 3 HD Humanoid.

   * **RHD:** 3d8  
   * **BAB/Saves:** From 3 levels of "Humanoid" (medium BAB, good Reflex).  
   * **Feats:** 2 (1 at 1st HD, 1 at 3rd HD).  
2. **Add Class Levels (Path 1):** You decide to make this Bugbear a **Level 2 Fighter**.

   * **Class Levels:** 2d10 (from Fighter)  
   * **BAB/Saves:** You add 2 levels of "Fighter" progression (fast BAB, good Fort).  
3. **The Result: The "Bugbear Fighter 2"**

   * **Total HD:** 5 (3 RHD \+ 2 Class Levels)  
   * **HP:** (3d8 \+ 2d10) \+ (5 x Con modifier)  
   * **BAB:** Add the BAB from 3 Humanoid RHD \+ 2 Fighter levels.  
   * **Saves:** Add the base saves from both sources (3 Humanoid \+ 2 Fighter).  
   * **Total Feats:** 3 (1 at 1st HD, 1 at 3rd HD, 1 at 5th HD). It also gets its Fighter bonus feats.  
   * **Total Skill Ranks:** Sum the ranks from its 3 RHD (Humanoid) and 2 Fighter levels.

This method is how you create the vast majority of unique, challenging "boss" monsters and specialized creatures in your game.
